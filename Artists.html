Artists

Leonardo da Vinci

Born:1452
Died:1519
A true Renaissance genius, Leonardo was not only a painter but also an inventor, scientist, and engineer.
Famous artworks include Mona Lisa and The Last Supper
His art is known for lifelike detail, perspective, and a mysterious quality that has fascinated viewers for centuries.
He filled notebooks with sketches of inventions, anatomy studies, and scientific observations.

Michelangelo

Born:1475
Died:1564
A towering figure of the Renaissance, Michelangelo was both a sculpture and a painter.
Famous artworks include the marble statueDavid and the painted ceiling of the Sistine Chapel in Rome.
His sculptures are admired for their realism and expressive detail.

Vincent van Gogh

Born:1853
Died:1890
A Dutch Post-Impressionist painter whose work is instantly recognizable for bold colors and swirling brushstrokes.
Famous artworks include Starry Night and Sunflowers
His art expressed deep emotion and personal struggle, often painting scenes of daily life and nature.
Though he sold very few works in his lifetime, van Gogh is now seen as one of the most influential artists in history.

Claude Monet

Born:1840
Died:1926
Founder of Impressionism, a movement that broke away from academic, realistic art.
Famous artworks include Water Lilies and Impression, Sunrise.
His style focused on capturing light, atmosphere, and momentary impressions rather than exact detail.
